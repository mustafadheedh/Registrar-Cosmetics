Compliance Services for the Cosmetics Sector

With industry-leading services and software solutions from Registrar Corp., you can ensure your cosmetics business complies with regulations and starts selling safe, effective goods.

Services

Registrar Corp is here to assist you in complying with changing cosmetic laws. We provide cosmetic and personal care companies with tech-enabled services that include everything from product listings and FDA facility registration to label review and adverse event reporting.

Registration of Facilities

The US FDA requires most cosmetic production facilities to register under MoCRA. Non-US facilities must assign a US Agent for FDA communication.

If your facilities are situated outside of the United States, we may assist you with registering them with the FDA and serve as your U.S. Agent.
Product Lists

The producer, packer, or distributor whose name appears on the label is required by the U.S. FDA to file a cosmetic product listing for the majority of cosmetic items. FDA cosmetic listings can be challenging and time-consuming.

We can assist you in quickly and easily listing your items.
Software for Reporting Adverse Events

The FDA requires notification of major adverse events related to US cosmetic use. Responsible parties must provide label copies, update info within a year, and allow FDA access during inspections.
In order to help ensure compliance, we provide Adverse Event Reporting Software.
Examining the label and ingredients

The Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FFDCA) prohibits the distribution of misbranded cosmetics with deceptive labeling. The Modernization of Cosmetics Act (MoCRA) mandates labeling criteria including adverse event contact and allergy labeling.
We can assist in making sure the labels on your cosmetic products comply.
Reports under the California Safe Cosmetics Act

Manufacturers, packers, and distributors selling products in California must comply with the California Safe Cosmetics Act (2005), disclosing over 3,000 potentially harmful components. Our experts can identify and report these components to the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) on your behalf.
Alerts for Imports



In the event of a Detention Without a Physical Examination (DWPE), obtain expert assistance. You can get assistance from Registrar Corp in identifying any noncompliance, recording corrective action, and submitting an accurate and comprehensive petition for removal.
Assistance with Detention

Registrar Corp may assist if your cosmetics cargo has been held up because of misbranding, adulteration, or a lack of compliance with rules. Together, you and our skilled team will pinpoint any possible noncompliance problems and create a remedial strategy.
